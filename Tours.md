# AfghanTours — Tours Instructions (Strict Enforcement)

## Purpose
Defines how the tours system should behave across:
- the tours listing page
- individual tour pages
- itinerary rendering
- route display
- accommodation and logistics sections
- public pricing presentation

Tours are the primary commercial product.

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Preserve route and overnight clarity.
- Keep the itinerary central.
- Do not expose internal costing in public-facing sections.
- Default public-facing tier to Standard.
- Use modular sections.
- Attractions in itinerary context should open the drawer by default.

---

## Tours Listing Page
Each tour card should include:
- hero image
- title
- short summary
- duration
- activity level
- from-price language
- primary destination / hub context

Cards must remain visually clean.

---

## Tour Detail Page Required Structure
1. Hero
2. Quick facts
3. Route snapshot
4. Overview
5. Day-by-day itinerary
6. Accommodation
7. Travel & logistics
8. Add-ons
9. Dates & pricing
10. FAQs

Do not reorder these casually without explicit instruction.

---

## Hero Section
Include:
- title
- summary
- duration
- activity level
- group size
- standard-tier public-facing price language
- CTA

---

## Route Snapshot
Must show:
- overnight hubs
- nights per hub
- route flow
- optional map

This section is mandatory because it reinforces the hub system.

---

## Itinerary Rules
Use:
- day cards or accordion sections
- concise narrative
- activities / attraction chips
- travel notes
- overnight location
- highlights box

The itinerary must be easy to scan.
No long dense text blocks.

---

## Itinerary Day Structure
Each day should include:
- day number + title
- short paragraph
- activities / attractions
- travel note
- overnight
- highlights

---

## Attraction Interaction
Attractions on itinerary days should:
- be clickable
- open the drawer
- support map sync when that system is active

Do not replace this with plain dead text unless explicitly instructed.

---

## Accommodation Section
Show:
- likely accommodation profile by hub
- tier-based expectations
- sample properties where useful

Do not guarantee exact hotel unless explicitly requested.

---

## Travel & Logistics Section
Show:
- main transport mode
- road or flight logic
- realistic access notes
- terrain / route context where relevant

This must feel operationally credible.

---

## Add-ons
Support modular add-on blocks such as:
- Dubai Escort Service
- domestic flight upgrades
- ski equipment
- clothing
- private guide support

Do not bury add-ons inside unrelated text.

---

## Dates & Pricing
Public-facing pricing should:
- default to Standard
- use “From $X,XXX per person”
- state that Budget / Premium / Luxury options are available
- encourage inquiries for custom pricing

Never expose internal cost math in this section.

---

## FAQs
Tour FAQs should reinforce trust and logistics clarity:
- comfort
- travel style
- route expectations
- access realism
- tier differences

---

## Visual Goal
Tour pages should feel like:
- premium expedition pages
- clear operating plans
- not generic package tours
- not raw CSV outputs
