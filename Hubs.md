# AfghanTours — Hubs Instructions (Strict Enforcement)

## Purpose
Defines how hub city pages should work.

Hubs are:
- overnight anchors
- logistics bases
- accommodation nodes
- transport nodes
- attraction staging points
- route logic centers

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Preserve the hub system.
- Do not turn hubs into generic city guide pages.
- Keep the operational role central.
- Include transport and accommodation logic.
- Use real hub codes and real operational behavior.

---

## Required Hub Page Structure
1. Hero
2. Operational role
3. Expertise / why AfghanTours uses this hub
4. Logistics
5. Accommodation profile
6. Transport access
7. Nearby attractions
8. Related tours

---

## Hero Rules
Include:
- name
- subtitle
- overview
- airport status if relevant

The hero should establish the hub as a serious operational base.

---

## Operational Role
Explain why the hub matters in the system.
This should sound strategic and operational, not generic.

---

## Expertise
Explain how AfghanTours specifically uses the hub.

This is one of the strongest trust-building sections on the site.

---

## Logistics Section
Should include:
- airport status
- road access
- corridor role
- whether the hub is primarily air-enabled or road-based

Do not imply airport functionality that the operating model does not actually use.

---

## Accommodation Profile
Must include:
- summary
- tier-based examples
- notes about expectations

Use only these tiers:
- budget
- standard
- premium
- luxury

Do not use VIP as a hotel tier.

Do not guarantee exact inventory unless explicitly requested.

---

## Transport Access
Explain:
- how people reach the hub
- whether flights are used
- whether it is mainly road-based
- what routes it anchors

---

## Nearby Attractions
Show a curated set of attractions linked to that hub.

---

## Related Tours
Show tours that:
- start there
- overnight there
- use it as a staging hub

---

## Visual Goal
Hub pages should feel:
- premium
- strategic
- grounded
- trustworthy
- clear
